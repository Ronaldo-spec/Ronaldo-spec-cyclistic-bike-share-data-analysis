# Summary Results

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|---|---:|
| Total trips after cleaning | 5,590,841 |
| Casual rider trips | 2,049,922 |
| Annual member trips | 3,540,919 |
| Average ride length - casual riders | 19.96 minutes |
| Average ride length - annual members | 11.83 minutes |
| Overall average ride length | 14.81 minutes |

## Findings

### 1. Members ride more frequently

Annual members recorded more trips than casual riders, with 3,540,919 trips compared with 2,049,922 casual rider trips.

### 2. Members show weekday and commuting behavior

Members are more active on weekdays and during typical commuting hours, especially around 07:00-09:00 and 16:00-18:00.

### 3. Casual riders are weekend and leisure-oriented

Casual riders are more active on weekends and during warmer months, especially from May to September.

### 4. Casual riders have longer average rides

Casual riders have an average ride length of 19.96 minutes, while members average 11.83 minutes.

### 5. Longer rides create a membership conversion opportunity

Casual riders are more represented in longer-duration trips, especially trips above 30 minutes. This segment can be targeted with cost-saving membership campaigns.

## Recommendations

### 1. Weekend and summer conversion campaign

Target casual riders during weekends and peak seasonal months with membership trials or summer promotions.

Example message:

> Try 1 Month Membership for Free This Weekend.

### 2. Time-and-duration-based targeting

Use app notifications or digital ads during casual rider active hours around 11:00-17:00.

Example message:

> Riding more than 20 minutes? Save more with a monthly membership.

### 3. Digital marketing based on rider behavior

Use email marketing, social media, and location-based content to highlight membership benefits for leisure rides, weekend trips, parks, and tourist areas.
